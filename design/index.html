What Does SwiftID Offer Small Business Owners?

SwiftID lets small business owners approve Spark Business account activity by responding to notifications on their mobile phone. By letting the bank know that they have possession of their phone and approve the activity, it helps our customers feel secure no matter what.

Scenario 1: Bill Pay (Channel: Mobile)

When a small business owner is at the office, they want to pay bills, so they can keep finances up-to-date and maintain good relationships with their business partners. Before the tranaction can complete, they have to approve or deny the activity to keep their account safe.

NOT SwiftID!

Use personal authentication like Password, TouchID, or SureSwipe.

SwiftID and One-Time-PIN pose a risk by initiating and approving an action on same device. This can be used by anyone with access to the mobile device.

Scenario 2: Delegated Administration (Channel: In-Branch / ATM / Web / Mobile)

When a small business employee is at an ATM, they want to withdraw money, so they can purchase supplies at a local market. Before the tranaction can complete, the small business owner has to approve or deny the activity to keep their account safe.

Scenario 3: Money Movement (Channel: Web / Mobile)

When a small business owner is at their work laptop, they want to move money from their business checking to their business savings account, so they can have a financial buffer in lean times. Before the tranaction can complete, they have to approve or deny the activity to keep their account safe.

Types of Authentication

Username and Password
  • A username is limited to 32 characters
  • A password is limited to 20 characters
  • A username or password can be valid, invalid, or expired and the server can be down
  • A customer is locked out of the account after 5 invalid attempts
  • A temporary password can help a customer get back into their account (retired soon)
Multifactor Authentication
  • A customer can be valid or invalid, and the server can be down
  • A multifactor authentication response can be valid or invalid, and the server can be down
  • A customer is locked out of the account after 5 invalid attempts
One-Time-PIN (OTP)
  • One-Time-PIN allows a maximum of 10 requests for a PIN every 24 hours
  • A PIN can be valid, invalid, or expired and the server can be down
  • A customer is locked out of the account after 5 invalid attempts
  • The email or phone number for One-Time-PIN can be invalid

Isolina (2006)

Physical interactive media installation
Tech: MaxMSP and Jitter with custom wiring and hardware, Illustrator, After Effects, InDesign, Maya
See: Read "Isolina" in PDF
Authored and produced award-winning interactive storytelling exhibit woven around a personal narrative of childhood illness.
As traumas fade and the past becomes distant, memories re-work themselves, fashion themselves into stories that uphold a sense of being at that moment. Freedom to explore devoid of expectation or chronology, this installation presents itself as just being. There is a tree for playing or ignoring. A bench for sitting, a story to read. Marbles to play with, and a story to watch on the wall. These activities are available, but none are necessary.
My thoughts on growing up are generally unfocused, making it hard to pin-point exact happenings, memories, and sequences of events. As such, participants are offered an opportunity to experience my story through this lens. Participants are encouraged to do as they please with the installation. They can hang out at the shelf near the main installation or just sit on the bench next to the shelf. On approach, the video behind the tree shows a default scene. This image pulses to and from white in a breath-like manner to indicate that Isolina is waiting. Her story is yet to be told.

Non-linear [working title]
(Concept only, 2005)

Concept for interactive media installation
Tech: Word processor, After Effects, Maya
This piece is made to be interrupted. It tells a story, visually, textually; but it does not invite quiet contemplation for the entire story. Quite contrary to the native gallery-goers instinct, the piece requires noise to tell you its story. It will not allow for peace -- though the story itself is sometimes peaceful -- it thrives on cellphones and loud talking, bumps and mis-interpreted meanings. Video on each screen appears to be both discrete and inter-related across all screens. Field audio plays from speakers within the screens, complementing the imagery. When users approach the screen, there is no immediate reaction from the video. But, noise of a certain loudness triggers a text overlay which appears as subtitling in white bars on the bottom of the screen.
A prototype application has been developed that explores the functionality of the installation running with interaction via a mock-up "voice actor", your own microphone, or no interaction at all (which allows for viewing of the entire narrative, un-interrupted).

dispersal_residue [working title] (2005)

Sound Installation
Tech: MaxMSP, Four-channel audio system
See: Video of Room-Scale Installation (03:40, 49MB MOV)
This installation is the culmination of work dealing with the feedback of the surrounding environment. A benign object exists in the middle of the room, humming and lowing. Once the audience creates a noise, the piece responds with altered versions of the same. Sound is passed from the user to the environment, to the machine and back out again in a natural cycle. The piece is one of self-awareness and play.
