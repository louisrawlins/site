HR Portal for Kaiser Permanente

Intranet redesign and style guide.

The Human Resources portal for Kaiser Permanente that serves over 160,000 employees across six regions, with 40,000 transactions per day. It had been created ten years earlier and was painful for employees to use.

Our challenge was to design an engaging, easy-to-use system for employees required to use the portal, while making content updates clear for our partners in Benefits, Retirement, and Payroll.

Style Guide

Because we knew we had to work with multiple groups, we focucsed our efforts on creating a style guide for the site. In order to facilitate collecting design decisions for developers and content strategists to reference, I created a style guide and hired an intern to help me complete it.

Design Research

We visited employees in company cafeterias, made appointments with administrators and hospital staff to learn how people were using the HR Portal and what we could do to improve.

Findability was key. People were having a difficult time sorting through all of the options. The order wasn't clear and there were so many things that an employee can do (and is required to do) on the HR Portal.

Bonfire School: Health, technology and design

How do we stay healthy?

I've been curious about the relationship between health and new technology, because computer use is frequently a sedentary activity.

The project began as a series of stories on Facebook to understand the community and evolved to include public classes.

Now, I'm researching wellbeing across cultures, to understand how restorative practices – the things we do as humans to regain equilibrium when we fall off-balance – are passed on to future generations.

Public Classes

These classes aim to make learning with an industry professional fun and engaging. Through a series of group exercises student are prompted to think about the needs of others and how we can solve their problems with software.

There are three main concepts in this series, each with it’s own class.
Learn to find human needs for software
Design software based on human needs
Build software with Python using design

In this series, student learn to listen and spend time with others to know what they need; we design software based on those needs and draw pictures to tell the story of what we can build; then we build software with Python and change it until it meets the needs we've identified.
