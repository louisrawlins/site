SwiftID for Small Business Banking

Art, copy and layout by Louis Rawlins

Purpose

National Small Business is adopting SwiftID as the preferred form of Multifactor Authentication for our small business customers. Because the needs of small business customers can be complicated, we are ensuring that we plan and understand SwiftID authentication across mobile and web.

An older authentication system that relies on personal Security Questions will be retired by July, 2016 making TouchID, SureSwipe, One-Time-PIN and SwiftID the alternative for Multifactor Authentication.

SwiftID makes security seamless for Spark Business customers. National Small Business supports our customers with authentication that helps them feel secure no matter what.

Timing

Design work for SwiftID in Spark Business must be complete before June, 2016.

How to Use This Document

This document is intended to map wireframe and comprehensive flows for Login on Spark Business for web and mobile. It describes what a small business owner may need to do, how we support them, and where there may be gaps between current and future experiences.

We are combining NATIVE MOBILE and RESPONSIVE WEB to illustrate interactions between different channels. For Spark Busines, we can consider that each channel include the specific platforms outlined to the right.

THIS DOCUMENT IS TO BE USED FOR REVIEW WITH DEVELOPMENT TEAM.

What Does SwiftID Offer Small Business Owners?

SwiftID lets small business owners approve Spark Business account activity by responding to notifications on their mobile phone. By letting the bank know that they have possession of their phone and approve the activity, it helps our customers feel secure no matter what.

Scenario 1: Bill Pay (Channel: Mobile)

When a small business owner is at the office, they want to pay bills, so they can keep finances up-to-date and maintain good relationships with their business partners. Before the tranaction can complete, they have to approve or deny the activity to keep their account safe.

NOT SwiftID!

Use personal authentication like Password, TouchID, or SureSwipe.

SwiftID and One-Time-PIN pose a risk by initiating and approving an action on same device. This can be used by anyone with access to the mobile device.

Scenario 2: Delegated Administration (Channel: In-Branch / ATM / Web / Mobile)

When a small business employee is at an ATM, they want to withdraw money, so they can purchase supplies at a local market. Before the tranaction can complete, the small business owner has to approve or deny the activity to keep their account safe.

Scenario 3: Money Movement (Channel: Web / Mobile)

When a small business owner is at their work laptop, they want to move money from their business checking to their business savings account, so they can have a financial buffer in lean times. Before the tranaction can complete, they have to approve or deny the activity to keep their account safe.

Types of Authentication

Username and Password
  • A username is limited to 32 characters
  • A password is limited to 20 characters
  • A username or password can be valid, invalid, or expired and the server can be down
  • A customer is locked out of the account after 5 invalid attempts
  • A temporary password can help a customer get back into their account (retired soon)
Multifactor Authentication
  • A customer can be valid or invalid, and the server can be down
  • A multifactor authentication response can be valid or invalid, and the server can be down
  • A customer is locked out of the account after 5 invalid attempts
One-Time-PIN (OTP)
  • One-Time-PIN allows a maximum of 10 requests for a PIN every 24 hours
  • A PIN can be valid, invalid, or expired and the server can be down
  • A customer is locked out of the account after 5 invalid attempts
  • The email or phone number for One-Time-PIN can be invalid
